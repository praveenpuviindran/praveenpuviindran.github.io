Post-Graduate Work

My post-graduate work focuses on large-scale machine learning in clinical and sports intelligence domains, bridging statistical modeling with real-world impact.

NIH — Postbaccalaureate Research Fellow (2025–Present)

Framingham Heart Study — Heavy Menstrual Bleeding (HMB) Proteomics

I develop Python workflows that analyze 3,000+ proteins across more than 25,000 participants to identify biomarkers for heavy menstrual bleeding. My work integrates feature engineering, pathway enrichment, and predictive modeling to improve diagnostic latency.

  • Built pipelines for large-scale proteomic ingestion & QC
  • Modeled 3K proteins using LASSO to derive proteomic risk scores
  • Integrated protein interaction networks to isolate causal mechanisms
  • Developing diagnostic classifiers for clinical deployment

NBA Synergy Engine — Moneyball 2.0

Permutation-Invariant Neural Network for Lineup Chemistry

A full-stack analytics system built to solve the NBA “fit problem.” I engineered a DeepSet neural network capable of predicting the net rating of any 5-man lineup using tracking data (2014–2025), and a generative module that selects the optimal 5th player for a team.

  • Created ETL pipelines ingesting 170,000+ possessions into a normalized SQL schema
  • Used GMM + PCA to derive 10 modern playstyle archetypes
  • Trained a PyTorch DeepSet model (RMSE ≈ 39.94) on 3,500+ lineup samples
  • Engineered a vectorized optimization engine (“AI GM”) that simulates all 450 players
  • Validated synergy predictions on real OKC Thunder rotations
