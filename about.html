About Me

I'm a data scientist and computational researcher focused on applying machine learning, quantitative modeling, and statistical inference to biomedical, clinical, and sports intelligence problems.

Background

I completed my B.S. in Statistics & Analytics at UNC Chapel Hill, where I conducted research in cancer pharmacology, virology, neurology, and medical imaging. My work ranged from wet-lab experimentation to computational modeling, building ML classifiers for RNA-seq tumor data, analyzing hippocampal dynamics with Dynamic Causal Modeling, and studying microbiome shifts in radiation oncology.

I now work as a Postbaccalaureate Research Fellow at the NIH Framingham Heart Study, where I build machine learning pipelines over 3,000 proteins in 25,000+ participants to develop biomarker-driven diagnostic tools.

Technical Skills

  • Python — pandas, NumPy, scikit-learn, PyTorch
  • R — tidyverse, caret
  • SQL, MATLAB
  • Machine Learning, Model Interpretability (SHAP)
  • ETL pipelines, reproducible modeling workflows
