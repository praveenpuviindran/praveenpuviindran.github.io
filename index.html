About Me

I'm an aspiring data scientist who works across biomedical research, clinical modeling, and sports analytics. I am passionate about using data to not only solve real-time problems, but also understand the past in a way to supplement our understanding of the present and future.

Background

I completed my B.S. in Statistics and Analytics at UNC Chapel Hill. I spent my undergrad working in various labs across cancer pharmacology, virology, neurology, RNA-seq modeling, and medical imaging. Some of that work involved wet-lab experiments like 20-day chemotherapy dosing studies to optimize dosing schedules and regimens. Other parts focused on computational modeling like tumor subtype classification for Kaposi Sarcoma and directional connectivity analysis from fMRI scans.

I currently work at the Framingham Heart Study as a postbaccalaureate research fellow with the National Institutes of Health. My work focuses on large-scale proteomics. My current project is analyzing data on nearly 3000 proteins measured across 25,000 participants. My goal is to identify biomarkers for heavy menstrual bleeding and build risk-score tools that shorten the time it takes for patients to get diagnosed.

I'm also a lifelong pianist and piano teacher. I grew up playing classical music and perform regularly. I also teach privately, focusing on technique and interpretation for students to build an emotional bond with the piano. Along with performing and teaching, I also arrange and write acapella and piano pieces that mix classical, South Asian, and Western styles. You can see my music work here: Piano Website.

Post-Graduate Work

NIH | Postbaccalaureate Research Fellow

At the Framingham Heart Study I work on proteomic modeling related to heavy menstrual bleeding. The goal is to identify biomarkers and build prediction tools that can support earlier diagnosis.

  • Building ETL pipelines for ~3000 proteins across 25,000 participants
  • Using LASSO, correlation networks, and pathway methods for feature selection
  • Developing early detection risk-score models utilizing a novel Protein Risk Score

NBA Synergy Engine

A lineup-chemistry modeling tool that uses tracking data to predict on-court synergy. The project includes a DeepSet model, shot-pattern clustering, and an AI GM that selects the best fifth player for any four-player core.

  • 170,000+ possessions processed with Python and SQL
  • PCA and Gaussian Mixture clustering for archetype modeling
  • DeepSet architecture built in PyTorch

Undergraduate Work

Kaposi Sarcoma RNA-seq Classifier

Built PCA and LASSO/SVM/RF/MLP models for HIV-positive tumor subtyping. Achieved ROC-AUC of 0.95. Identified a six-gene panel using RFE and SHAP.

Cancer Pharmacology

Ran 20-day chemotherapy dosing studies in lung cancer cell lines and modeled viability curves using R and Mathematica.

Neurology

Used SPM to analyze directional connectivity among hippocampal regions in epilepsy using dynamic causal modeling.

Oncology Microbiome

Analyzed 16S datasets using QIIME2 to study radiation-induced microbiome changes. Ran PERMANOVA and PCoA analyses.

Oncology Telehealth

Worked on a qualitative study of virtual oncology visits among Black adults. Co-author on a peer-reviewed publication.

Projects

Early Diabetes Risk Prediction

Engineered features for 253k BRFSS records. Random Forest with SMOTE reached 85 percent accuracy.

NBA Game Outcome Forecasting

Predicted game statistics across 1100+ NBA games. Achieved R² of 0.837 and MAE below 1 for several metrics.

Billiards Regression Study

Analyzed 150 games of 9-ball to study accuracy patterns and game flow. Found a consistent mid-game increase in shot accuracy.

Evolution of NBA Offense

Studied 40 years of league trends and found that three-point efficiency has become a stronger predictor of success than defensive metrics.

Crime and SAT Scores

Found that violent crime rates correlate around −0.45 with SAT performance, while non-violent crime shows little effect.
