Background

I started undergrad without a clear picture of exactly what I wanted to do long term. I was interested in science, problem-solving, and research, but it was not until I declared Statistics and Analytics at UNC Chapel Hill that things really clicked.

Once I began working with real datasets, I fell in love with the process of turning messy data into structure, then structure into insight. That led me into research roles across pharmacology, neurology, pathology, oncology, and computational biology, where I built both technical depth and domain awareness.

Today, I approach work as a data scientist focused on rigorous modeling, reproducible workflows, and clear communication. My interests span biomedical research, sports analytics, and other complex systems where data can drive better decisions.

My Workflow

  • Set the goal: Define the question and what decision the project should support.
  • Review the data: Check quality, gaps, and whether the data can answer the question.
  • Prepare the inputs: Clean and combine data, then build useful features.
  • Test model options: Compare approaches and choose one that is accurate and explainable.
  • Check real-world reliability: Evaluate on unseen data and study where errors happen.
  • Share and refine: Communicate results clearly, gather feedback, and improve the approach.

Built an early screening model using BRFSS data to identify high-risk individuals before diagnosis.
