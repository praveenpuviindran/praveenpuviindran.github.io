About Me

I'm a data scientist and computational researcher focused on applying machine learning, quantitative modeling, and statistical inference to biomedical, clinical, and sports-intelligence problems. I enjoy building end-to-end pipelines that translate high-dimensional datasets into real-world insights and actionable decision-making tools.

Background

I completed my B.S. in Statistics & Analytics at UNC Chapel Hill, where I conducted research in cancer pharmacology, virology, neurology, RNA-seq modeling, and medical imaging. My work bridged wet-lab and computational science — from 20-day drug-dosing experiments to building machine learning classifiers for tumor subtypes and modeling directional brain connectivity using fMRI.

I now work as a Postbaccalaureate Research Fellow at the NIH Framingham Heart Study, where I analyze large-scale proteomic datasets (3,000+ proteins across 25,000+ participants) to identify biomarkers, uncover causal pathways, and build risk-score systems for heavy menstrual bleeding.

Outside of research, I am a lifelong pianist and piano teacher. I perform classical repertoire, arrange fusion pieces blending Tamil and Western styles, and teach students technique, expression, and musical interpretation. You can explore my musical background here: Piano Website.
