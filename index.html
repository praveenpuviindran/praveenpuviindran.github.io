About Me

I'm a data scientist who works across biomedical research, clinical modeling, and sports analytics. I care about clean code, grounded methods, and building things that actually help people make decisions.

Background

I completed my B.S. in Statistics and Analytics at UNC Chapel Hill. I spent my undergrad working in cancer pharmacology, virology, neurology, RNA-seq modeling, and medical imaging. Some of that work involved wet-lab experiments like 20-day chemotherapy dosing studies. Other parts focused on computational modeling like tumor subtype classification and directional connectivity analysis in fMRI.

I now work at the Framingham Heart Study as a postbaccalaureate research fellow. My work focuses on large-scale proteomics. I analyze data on more than 3000 proteins measured across 25,000 participants. My goal is to identify biomarkers for heavy menstrual bleeding and build risk-score tools that shorten the time it takes for patients to get diagnosed.

I'm also a pianist and piano teacher. I grew up playing classical music and still perform regularly. I also teach technique and interpretation, help students prepare for auditions, and arrange pieces that mix classical, Tamil, and Western styles. You can see my music work here: Piano Website.
